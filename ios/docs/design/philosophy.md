# Handoff — Visual Philosophy

## Movement

**Terminal Kinetics.**

## Manifesto

Handoff is a tool about a moment, not a place. The moment your session crosses from a Mac to a phone. Most terminal apps render stillness — a prompt, a cursor, a box of stopped time. Handoff renders the in-between: the instant a session is halfway between two hands.

The icon is built from a single primitive — the chevron `❯` — the shortest, most universal glyph that says "terminal." Nothing else is needed. No screens, no silhouettes, no device frames, no baton, no arrow, no clever pictography. One shape, repeated with intention. Everything the icon communicates, it communicates through that repetition and the geometry of its displacement.

Color is functional, not decorative. Two values carry the entire story: GitHub blue (#58A6FF) for the session as it lived on the Mac — present but receding — and terminal green (#3FB950) for the same session, alive and forward, now on the phone. The background is not black but a deep graphite gradient, the same palette the app itself uses — so the icon and the app are the same visual family, not a marketing image bolted onto a utility.

The craftsmanship lives in what is absent. No drop shadow. No bevel. No gloss. No stacked layers. No ornament. No clever glyph inside a rectangle. The chevrons meet the background with rounded caps and a single breath of green glow that says: this one is current. Everything else is restraint. The icon should read at 1024 pixels as a sophisticated piece of identity work, and at 20 pixels as one unambiguous, right-pointing chevron. At every scale in between, the story of the handoff survives as a silhouette with a quiet echo.

Forward motion is implied, never drawn. No speed lines. No streaks. No particles. Displacement alone carries the kinesis — the way Muybridge proved a running horse leaves all four feet off the ground without needing to animate him. Two chevrons, offset and partially faded, are enough. The viewer's eye assembles the verb.

The final object must feel inevitable. As if there was only ever one right way to make this icon, and this is it.

## Principles

- **One shape only.** The chevron does everything.
- **Two colors only.** Blue = past. Green = present.
- **Displacement is the verb.** Offset communicates motion without decoration.
- **Background is app, not marketing.** Same palette, same family.
- **Silhouette survives.** At 20px, still a chevron. At 1024px, still a chevron.
- **No ornament, no bevel, no gloss.** The restraint is the point.
